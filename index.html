METHOD
HOW TO READ — Bar height = r (Pearson or Spearman). Range −1 to +1. Solid bars = statistically significant (p<0.05). Faded bars = not significant — disregard the value regardless of height. Near +1 = indices moved together that window. Near 0 = moved independently. Negative = moved in opposite directions.   Stability σ = std dev of rolling r over the full period — low σ means the relationship is consistent over time, high σ means it switches between regimes.

LAG / LEAD — X-axis = lag in days (−10 to +10). Y-axis = r at that lag. Peak bar = optimal lag. If peak is at +2, Index A today predicts Index B in 2 days — a potential timing edge. Peak at 0 = synchronous, no timing advantage. A lag gain of <0.05 over sync r is noise; >0.10 is worth investigating.   SCATTER — Each dot = one day's log returns. Orange line = regression. R² = variance explained (0.55 = one index explains 55% of the other's daily moves). β slope = how much B moves per unit of A. Tight cluster = reliable. Dispersed cloud = weak regardless of r.
