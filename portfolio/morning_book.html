Methodology notes

honest limitations

Destination flexibility — Valued as a call on the max of N correlated destinations minus the contracted netback. For N=2 we use the Margrabe exchange option (closed-form). For N≥3 we compound the Margrabe iteratively using Clark's approximation — accurate for low correlations, conservative when all destinations move together.

Volume flexibility — Valued as a Bachelier straddle on the per-MMBtu margin over the flex range. Bachelier (not Black) because the margin can go negative. Intrinsic = flex_volume × max(0, |current_margin|); extrinsic = flex_volume × |ATM straddle − |current_margin||.

Cancellation rights — The right to cancel N cargoes is a Bachelier put on the cargo margin with strike = 0. Intrinsic = N × max(0, −current_margin) — you only cancel when you're losing. Extrinsic = N × put_value − intrinsic, representing the value of the wait-and-see option.

Reload optionality — Margrabe on (reload_FOB_netback − original_DES_netback). Reload cost subtracts from both intrinsic and extrinsic.

Numbers are indicative. Closed-form formulas under-state deep optionality in highly-correlated markets and don't capture path-dependent features (early exercise, time-spread rolls, seasonal storage). For gas-to-power portfolio valuation with full American-exercise treatment, use a Monte Carlo engine. This module is best used to rank flex rights against each other, not to book P&L.

How it's computed

closed-form, no Monte Carlo

Intrinsic per destination = DES_dest − Freight(origin→dest) − Basis. Best one wins. Already captured in the FOB price the seller quotes — NOT part of the premium.

Extrinsic = value of the right to switch between destinations as prices evolve. Closed-form Margrabe (1978): for each alternative destination, value of switching from best is S_alt·N(d1) − S_best·N(d2) where d1=(ln(S_alt/S_best)+½σ²T)/(σ√T) and σ=√(σ1²+σ2²−2ρσ1σ2). For N≥3, sum N−1 pairwise terms. Vols from getEffectiveVol layer (override > realized > default), correlation from MKT_CORR.

Fair FOB Premium = Extrinsic only. This is the additional $/MMBtu the seller can quote on top of (cost basis + best intrinsic netback) to be paid for the option, not for the underlying delivery.

For Sabine cargoes with NWE + JKTC + MEI as live arbs, expect Margrabe extrinsic ≈ $0.20–0.60/MMBtu in normal markets, more when JKM-TTF spread is volatile or T is long. Conservative for highly correlated destinations (true max-of-N value > sum of pairwise Margrabe).
